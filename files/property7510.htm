Group/Layer/Partition Property Editor

Controls the visibility, renderability, selectability, and animation ghosting of members of groups, layers, and partitions.

To create a group: Select one or more objects, then click Group on the Edit panel. For more information about groups, see Grouping Objects [Scene Elements].

To create a layer: Open the scene layer manager (press 6) and then choose Layers New Layer or New Layer from Selection. For more information about layers, see Scene Layers [Scene Elements].

To create a partition: Select a pass, then choose Pass Partition New from the Render toolbar. For more information about partitions, see Passes & Partitions [Rendering].

To redisplay: In an explorer, click the icon for a group (press S to set the explorer's scope to Scene), layer (press L to set the scope to Layers), or pass (press P to set the scope to Passes).

Name

The name of the group, layer, or partition.

Layer Group Name

The name of the scene layer group to which the layer belongs. This parameter is available for scene layers only.

View Visibility

Determines whether objects are visible in the 3D views. Invisible objects can still be rendered according to their Render Visibility settings.

  • Hide members: Members are not displayed. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the View Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Show members: Members are displayed. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the View Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • No effect on members: Members are displayed or not displayed according to the View Visibility settings in their individual Visibility properties. The group/layer/partition does not override the visibility of members.

Render Visibility

Determines whether objects are visible during rendering. Non-renderable objects can still be seen in 3D views according to their View Visibility settings.

  • Hide members: Members are not rendered. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Render Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Show members: Members are rendered. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Render Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • No effect on members: Members are rendered or not rendered according to the Render Visibility settings in their individual Visibility properties. The group/layer/partition does not override the visibility of members.

Selectability

Determines whether objects are selectable in the 3D views. Objects are always selectable in explorer and schematic views.

  • Do not allow selecting members: Members are not selectable. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Selectability settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Allow selecting members: Members are selectable. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Selectability settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • No effect on members: Members are selectable or not selectable according to the Selectability settings in their individual Visibility properties. The group/layer/partition does not override the visibility of members.

View Playback Visibility

Determines whether objects are visible in the 3D views during playback. Hiding objects can increase playback speed.

  • Hide members: Members are not displayed during playback. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the View Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Show members: Members are displayed during playback. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the View Visibility settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Same as view visibility: Members are displayed or not displayed during playback according to the View Visibility setting of the group, layer, or partition (above).

  • No effect on members: Members are displayed or not displayed during playback according to the View Visibility settings in their individual Visibility properties. The group/layer/partition does not override the visibility of members.

Animation Ghosting

Determines whether the animation of objects is ghosted in the 3D views. Animation ghosting is sometimes called onion-skinning. For more information about ghosting, see Ghosting Animated Objects [Animation].

  • Do not ghost members: Members are not ghosted. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Animation Ghosting settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • Ghost members: Members are ghosted. The group/layer/partition setting overrides the Animation Ghosting settings in the members' individual Visibility properties.

  • No effect on members: Members are ghosted or not according to the Animation Ghosting settings in their individual Visibility properties. The group/layer/partition does not override the members' Visibility properties.
